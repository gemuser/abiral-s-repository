About

→ I am Abiral Pokharel, a product of the Nepali hills and the monsoon-scented winds. Born on June 29, 2006, in the tranquil town of Okhaldhunga, I carry the whispers of that place, the rustling leaves, the laughter of childhood friends as cherished memories. My roots dig deep into the soil of Kharpa, a village nestled among terraced fields in Khotang district. There, time flowed differently the sun painted the sky, and community bonds were woven like the threads of a traditional dhaka fabric. But life, like a meandering river, led me downstream. Now, I find myself in Biratnagar, Morang, a bustling city where tradition dances with modernity. The streets pulse with commerce, and the air hums with dreams. At 18 years old, I stand at the crossroads, eager to explore the uncharted paths ahead.
